# Features/ReactionCam/ — driver PiP (Phase 6 — built)

Concept: tap the CAM button on the iPad (toggle — tap again to drop it) → circular PiP appears on the TV showing that player's driver reacting to live physics. Dev/sim shortcut: launch arg `--show-cams` forces every PiP on.

Files (as built 2026-07-18)
- `ReactionCamView.swift` — circular-masked mini `RealityView`, framed as a look into the car's **cockpit**: driver bust (Quaternius rig, painted by `DriverPainter` from `design.driver` — the kid's character colors, hats and all, since C4/C5) + `PerspectiveCamera`, key + cool rim light, player-colored ring, name capsule. The bust rolls/glances into turns with the live car (`director.lean` × RaceTuning.reactionLeanAngle). Three layers, all in this file:
  - `WindshieldView` (behind) — sky, a road rushing at the camera, roadside posts, and the cabin (roof lining + A-pillars) framing the glass. The vanishing point slides *opposite* the turn (`cockpitVanishShift`) — that's what makes a corner feel like a corner — and the dashes scroll with the car's real speed. Overhead lights sweep across the glass faster the quicker you go.
  - `SteeringWheelView` (in front of the driver) — the car's own wheel, counter-turning with the live yaw rate (`-lean × cockpitWheelAngle`; a wheel turns much further than the car does, and that exaggeration is what reads at 180 pt). Shape comes from the chassis — `cockpitWheelSpokes` / `cockpitWheelRimWidth` / `cockpitWheelFlatBottom` give muscle a fat two-spoke bar, formula a flat-bottom three-spoke tiller, drift a skinny four-spoke — and colours from the car's paint (`partColors[wheels]` rim accent, body colour hub). No hands on the wheel: the standing rig has no seated arm pose and the wheel alone sells it.
  - **One `daylight` colour** (`ReactionState.daylight(carTint:)`) drives the sky wash, the cabin trim bounce **and** the 3D key light, so a boost warms the driver's face the same frame it warms the glass: track-day blue → boost orange → crash red → the car's own colour on the podium. The key light is washed toward white first (`cockpitKeyLightWash`) — a fully saturated key flattens the low-poly rig into single-colour slabs.
  
  Boost/celebrate spring-pops the whole PiP; crash knocks it askew. Positioned bottom-left/right per player by ArenaView. Face bubble uses the character's skin tone + face paint (`driver.faceDrawingPNG`, falling back to the legacy `CarDesign.faceDrawingPNG`).
- `ReactionDirector.swift` — race events → reaction state machine: `idle → steerLeft/steerRight → braced → boosted / crashed / celebrating`. Continuous inputs: yaw rate (lean) + loop-within-0.5 s (brace) + speed01; also exposes smoothed `lean` (−1…1) and `speed01` readouts for the PiP (blend rate RaceTuning.reactionMotionSmoothing). Discrete events override instantly. Min state hold (RaceTuning.reactionMinHold) so it never flickers; celebrating is sticky. Unit-tested.
- `ReactionFeed.swift` — bridges live `RaceSession` state → one director per racer, every frame from ArenaView's scene subscription. Discrete events detected by diffing racer stats (crashes↑ = crashed, meter 1→0 = boosted, finishTime set = celebrating) — no extra event plumbing.
- `DriverPoser.swift` — `make(profile:)` builds the bust painted by `DriverPainter`, then plays the matching Quaternius clip (idle/Punch/Death/Jump → idle/boosted/crashed/celebrating), crossfaded 0.15 s. Clips are separate USDZs (`driver-*.usdz`) from `tools/convert_driver_rig.py`; skeletons match so any clip plays on the one bust. **The bust ships on the idle rig alone and the event clips stream in behind it** (`loadEventClips`) — awaiting all four rigged USDZs up front (they load serially on the main actor) left the PiP an empty circle for ~14 s on a cold launch. `apply` falls back to the idle clip for anything not in yet, so arriving early only costs a plain-faced boost for a moment.
- `FaceDecals.swift` — face expression per state: hand-drawn vector paths composited over the PiP instead of the planned texture-swap quad — reads better at PiP size, zero texture authoring, and no emoji (CLAUDE.md). Steering/braced states reuse the idle clip; the face carries them.

Live-editing the driver (C6): the character editor renders this same PiP while a kid picks hair and hats, so `ReactionCamView` swaps its bust when `design.driver` changes (`rebuildIfDriverChanged`, guarded by `DriverPainter.appearanceSignature`). It **rebuilds** rather than repaints: `DriverPainter.apply` on a bust that's already mid-animation swaps the wardrobe geometry but leaves the stripe texture stale, so a kid going bald kept their hair colour. A fresh poser is cheap — AssetStore caches the rig, DriverPainter caches the texture. Races never hit this path; the driver can't change mid-lap.

**Framing chases the head, and must keep doing so** (`DriverPoser.frameOnHead`). The PiP used to aim its camera at a constant height (`driverSourceHeight × driverHeadHeightRatio`) and that broke three separate ways at once: the reaction clips translate the rig's ROOT, so the driver walked out of a fixed frame (you got a shirt, then a pair of hips); body types rescale the rig, so two cars framed two different crops of two differently-sized people; and the rig heights differ per character anyway. The fix aims at the **posed Head joint** — the same joint the hats already ride, via `HeadPinSystem.headPosition` — and infers the rig's height from where that head sits. Three rules it must keep:

1. **Never measure the rig from `visualBounds`.** Called at build time it races the load (the PiP that lost the race framed a giant pair of hips while its twin framed a face); it also includes flung limbs and props. Height is read off the head joint instead, latched once and only while idle — read mid-cheer, a raised skull scales the rig up and parks the camera in the driver's lap.
2. **When the joint lookup fails, hold the last good framing.** It returns nil for a frame or two on every clip crossfade. The bind-pose fallback is a WORLD point the animated root has already left, so snapping to it is what dropped the camera into the driver's hips a second after it had framed their face.
3. **Drive it from `SceneEvents.Update`, not RealityView's `update` closure.** The closure only fires when the director publishes a change, and the instant a racer finishes it stops changing — while the cheer clip plays on and jumps the driver clean out of the frozen framing. For the same reason the framing state lives on `DriverPoser` (a class) and the poser lives in a `PoserBox`, not in SwiftUI `@State`: state written from a long-lived RealityKit closure and read back through `@State` goes stale, and the chase never converges.

Deviations from the original plan
- No Blender-authored seated/lean/brace poses: the rig's stock clips + face badges cover every state. In-car "seated" pose = standing rig sunk hip-deep into the chassis (legs hidden — RaceTuning.driverSinkRatio).
- Sprite fallback not needed in Simulator; profile the second RealityView on real Apple TV hardware before shipping (fallback plan unchanged if it costs > 10% frame time).
