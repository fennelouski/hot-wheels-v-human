# Core/TrackKit/ ‚Äî blueprint ‚Üí validated layout ‚Üí 3D track (Phase 1, built)

Files (as built ‚Äî geometry facts in PieceCatalog.swift's header comment)
- `PieceType.swift` ‚Äî enum: `startGate, finishGate, straight, curve90L, curve90R, curveLarge, hillUp, hillDown, bump, loop, rampJump`. Raw values are wire format.
- `PieceCatalog.swift` ‚Äî `TrackPieceDefinition` table. Kenney pieces are **not** grid-uniform, so each definition carries measured real-valued offsets in a "traversal frame" (entry at origin, travel +Z, surface y = 0): `exitOffset`, `headingChange`, nominal `FootprintRect`, and a `CenterlineShape` (line/arc/profiled/crest/verticalLoop) for spline generation. Gates = straight + gate-arch overlay model. `curve90L` and `hillDown` reuse the R/up models traversed in reverse.
  - **Hills are role-dependent.** A hill's geometry depends on its neighbours, so `TrackLayoutSolver` resolves each *run* of consecutive same-direction hills and calls `definition(for:role:)` (`HillRole.solo/entry/middle/exit`) ‚Äî the dictionary entry is the `.solo` form. A lone hill stays the one-piece `hill-complete` (S-curve, level at both ends, +1 level over 0.8 m). A run lays out like real track: `hill-beginning` bends into the slope, plain `track-wide-straight` pitched by `RaceTuning.hillRunSlope` (30¬∞) rides down the middle at +2 levels per 0.8¬∑cos30 m of advance, and `hill-end` flattens out. Chaining `hill-complete` instead gave a staircase of humps, because it returns to level at every seam.
  - `.profiled` carries the mesh's **measured** bed as normalised (t, u) samples. Hills used a straight `.line` chord across an S-curved bed, which floated cars ~26 mm over it and sank them ~29 mm through it. Hill meshes also have a recessed connector tongue, so their bed lift is 0.18 m, not the flat pieces' 0.19 m.
- `BlueprintValidator.swift` ‚Äî non-empty; ‚â§ 40 pieces; exactly one `startGate` at index 0; at most one `finishGate`, last (sprint) OR exit meets start (circuit); no negative elevation; footprint overlap per elevation level via axis-aligned world rects (headings are always 90¬∞ multiples), skipping the loop because it's an overpass ‚Äî it advances 0 m, so the straights it joins always sit under its plate and its arc. Kid-readable reasons. Uses the solver's placements so the two can never disagree.
- `TrackLayoutSolver.swift` ‚Äî accumulates (position, yaw, level) per segment ‚Üí `PlacedPiece[]` + `LaneSplines` (center/left/right + per-waypoint unit `laterals`, ~0.1 m spacing, lane offsets per `RaceTuning.laneOffsetWide`, tapered to center through the narrow loop). `laterals` is the track frame's "left" vector ‚Äî the rail follower derives its up vector from `cross(tangent, lateral)`, which is what caught the long-standing mirrored z-sign in the arc laterals (the "left" lane used to sweep across to the right through every curve and jog back at the exit seam). Pure math, no RealityKit.
- `TrackSpawner.swift` ‚Äî RealityKit: loads models via `AssetStore`, places them, `generateStaticMesh` collision per model part (convex hulls would seal the loop), tags gate overlays with `CheckpointComponent`. Cars collide with SOLVED geometry, not the visual meshes: flat pieces get one bed slab (pitched along their rise), and anything whose bed a slab can't describe ‚Äî the loop's ring and the `.profiled` hills, whose measured bed bows up to 7 cm off its own chord ‚Äî gets `splineCollision`, a chain of boxes riding the piece's own centreline with `cross(tangent, lateral)` for up (the same track frame the rail follower rolls cars with). Only the jump ramp keeps its exact mesh. Cosmetic `supports*` legs under elevated pieces: the models are authored exactly one elevation level tall (0.2 m) with the base at the origin, so a piece at `entryLevel` N stacks N of them at its footprint centre ‚Äî no scaling. Narrow-model pieces (the loop) get `supports`, the rest `supports-wide`. Legs are collision-free (a car flying off must fall past them) and hills are skipped ‚Äî their bed slopes through the piece, so a full-height post would pierce a `hillDown`, and the flat neighbours at each end carry the legs.
- `AssetStore.swift` ‚Äî `@MainActor` class (not an actor: Entity is MainActor-bound), async `entity(named:)`, cache + clone-on-vend.
- `RandomTrackGenerator.swift` ‚Äî NOT built yet; arrives with the "shuffle" button in Phase 5 (BUILD-ORDER).
